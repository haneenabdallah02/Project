Ways to Obtain Musical Theatre Show Tickets

1. Online Ticket Platforms

One of the most convenient ways to purchase musical theatre show tickets is through online ticket platforms such as Ticketmaster, StubHub, or Broadway.com. These platforms provide a wide selection of shows and seating options.

Tip: To secure the cheapest tickets from these websites, look for tickets during the months of July, August, January, February, or March. Ticket prices also tend to drop as the day of the show gets closer.

2. Box Office

Visit the box office of the theatre where the musical is being performed. Purchasing tickets directly from the box office can sometimes offer discounts and the opportunity to choose specific seats. Buying a ticket through Ticketmaster, StubHub, or Broadway.com include a convenience fee. If bought directly from the box office, one does not have to pay this fee and can save $19-$30 per ticket.

3. Theatre Membership Programs

Consider joining membership programs offered by theatres or production companies. Members often enjoy benefits such as early access to tickets, discounts, and exclusive presales. The Theatre Development Fund (TDF) program requires a $30 annual fee in exchange for purchasing tickets anywhere from $11 - $60.

Visit the TDF website to determine your eligibility for membership!

4. Discount Ticket Services

Explore discount ticket services like TKTS booths in major cities, offering same-day discounted tickets for Broadway and off-Broadway shows. Additionally, some websites specialize in offering discounted theatre tickets. Broadway lotteries are also a great option to get majorly discounted tickets. This works by entering for a chance to purchase discounted tickets. Tickets must then be picked up at the box office by the winner with an ID. Winners are randomly selected and have an hour to claim their tickets after they are notified. Some of the most popular lotteries include Broadway Lottery Direct and Lucky Seat.

Pros: Tickets can cost anywhere from $10-$60

Cons: Winners do not know what seat they won until after purchasing and picking up their tickets at the box office. Tickets are also non-transferable, non-refundable, and cannot be resold. Only 1-2 tickets can be won per lottery ticket.
