Alternative Options

Alternative Options!

Shows available for streaming:

The Phantom of the Opera

The Phantom of the Opera is about a disfigured, murderous musical genius that haunts the Paris Opera House. He becomes mesmerized by the beauty and voice of soprano Christine Daaé, who is an orphan. He lures her into his protégé by pretending to be her angel of music, sent by her late father. The Phantom fiercely falls in love with her, while she is both in awe and afraid of him. You can stream this timeless masterpiece for free on YouTube!

Rent

Rent follows a group of friends in New York City, capturing their lives as they navigate love, friendship, and the challenges of living with HIV/AIDS during the 1980s. This show expresses the frustration and aftermath of the first wave of the AIDS crisis, in which the main characters are HIV positive and shine light on their perspectives. You can watch the movie adaptation, or the recorded live production on Amazon Prime Video.

Newsies

Newsies tells the story of the Newsboy's Strike of 1899 in New York City. A group of newsboys, led by the charismatic Jack Kelly, stand up against the newspaper publishers to protest fair working conditions and rights. You can stream this recorded production on Disney Plus.

Hamilton

This production chronicles the life of Alexander Hamilton, one of the United States' Founding Fathers. Through hip-hop, R&B, and traditional musical styles, this show highlights the orphaned immigrant's importance in American history. You can stream this musical on Disney Plus.

Miss Saigon

Miss Saigon explores the tragic love story between American GI Chris and Vietnamese orphan Kim during the Vietnam War. The 25th anniversary performance can be streamed on Amazon Prime Video.

Cats

Cats revolves around a tribe of Jellicle cats that gather for their annual Jellicle Ball, where one cat is chosen to ascend to the Heaviside Layer and be reborn. This production can be streamed on Amazon Prime Video.




Autism-Friendly Performances

The Theatre Development Fund (TDF) has announced the 12th season of performances that are autism-friendly (AFP) for those with autism and cognitive disabilities. Check out the schedule of the planned performances by visiting the TDF website.
Stay informed about upcoming dates tailored to provide an inclusive experience for all audiences.
